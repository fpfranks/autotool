========================================================
FINDING YOUR FIRST 50 UK LEADS — FREE METHODS
AI Lead Reply Bot | Lead Generation Guide
========================================================

OVERVIEW
---------
This guide focuses on three starter niches that are easiest to prospect quickly:
1. Car Garages / MOT Centres
2. Hair Salons / Barbers
3. Estate Agents

These niches were chosen because:
- They have high volumes of local businesses across every UK city and town
- They're easy to find contact details for (most have websites and Google profiles)
- The pain point (missed enquiries) is obvious and immediately relatable
- Decision makers are usually the owner — no corporate gatekeeping

Target: 50 qualified leads in your first week. At 10 per day for 5 days, this is very achievable.

========================================================
NICHE 1 — CAR GARAGES / MOT CENTRES
========================================================

WHAT MAKES A GOOD PROSPECT:
- Independent garage (not a franchise like Kwik Fit or Halfords)
- 4+ star Google rating with 30+ reviews (established, cares about reputation)
- Website with a contact form (not just a phone number)
- No visible live chat or chatbot on their website
- Active on Google Maps (profile photo, photos of the garage)
- Ideally NOT using an online booking system already — this means they're still manual

GOOGLE MAPS SEARCH QUERIES TO USE:
Open maps.google.com, make sure your search location is set to a UK city you're targeting.

Copy-paste these searches one at a time:
1. "independent garage" [city name]
2. "MOT centre" [city name]
3. "car service" [city name]
4. "auto repair" [city name]
5. "tyre fitting" [city name]

Best UK cities to start with (high density of independent garages):
- Birmingham, Manchester, Leeds, Sheffield, Bristol, Liverpool, Nottingham, Leicester, Glasgow, Cardiff

HOW TO WORK THROUGH THE RESULTS:
1. Open Google Maps with the search query above
2. Click each result in the left panel
3. Check: Is it independent? (Not a franchise)
4. Look at star rating — aim for 3.9+ stars with 20+ reviews
5. Click "Website" on their Google listing
6. On their website, check:
   - Is there a contact form? → GOOD
   - Is there a live chat widget (Intercom, Tawk, Drift)? → SKIP (already covered)
   - Is there an online booking system? → SKIP (already has automation)
7. If it looks like a good prospect: find their email (see below)

HOW TO FIND THEIR EMAIL:
Most small garages put their email on their website. Check these pages:
- "Contact us" page
- Footer of the website (scroll to the bottom)
- "About us" page
- Google Maps listing → under the address, sometimes shows email

If no email is visible on the website:
- Try [name]@[domain].co.uk (e.g. dave@cityautocentre.co.uk)
- Try info@[domain].co.uk
- Try hello@[domain].co.uk
- Use Hunter.io (free tier: 25 lookups/month) — enter their domain to find emails

If you still can't find an email:
- Note the business in your sheet with a "no email found" note
- Skip for now — don't waste time. Move on to the next one.

QUALIFYING CHECKLIST — GARAGE:
[ ] Independent (not a franchise)
[ ] 3.9+ star rating
[ ] Has a website with contact form
[ ] No live chat visible
[ ] No online booking visible
[ ] Email found
→ If all ticked: ADD TO SHEET

EXAMPLE SEARCH SESSION (30 min → 10 prospects):
- Search "independent garage Birmingham" on Google Maps
- Work through results top to bottom
- Spend max 3 minutes per prospect on the above checks
- Record in your Google Sheet as you go
- Move to "MOT centre Birmingham" next

========================================================
NICHE 2 — HAIR SALONS / BARBERS
========================================================

WHAT MAKES A GOOD PROSPECT:
- Independent salon or barber (not a chain like Supercuts or Rush)
- Has Instagram or Facebook with active posting (engaged audience)
- Website with a contact/booking form OR takes DMs for bookings
- No booking system visible (no Fresha, Booksy, Treatwell widget on their site)
- 4+ star Google rating
- 1–5 stylists (small enough that the owner still handles enquiries personally)

WHY TO AVOID ONES WITH FRESHA/BOOKSY:
Salons already on Fresha or Booksy have automated their bookings. They're less likely to need your bot and more likely to say "we already have that." Save your time.

How to tell if they're on a booking system:
- "Book online" button that links to fresha.com, booksy.com, treatwell.co.uk, or vagaro.com
- If you see these: mark as "has booking system" in notes and skip

GOOGLE MAPS SEARCH QUERIES:
1. "hair salon" [city name]
2. "barber" [city name]
3. "ladies hairdresser" [city name]
4. "gents barbers" [city name]
5. "men's grooming" [city name]

SOCIAL MEDIA PROSPECTING (bonus — often faster for salons):
Many salons are more active on Instagram than they are on their website.

Method:
1. Open Instagram
2. Search: "#[cityname]hairdresser" or "#[cityname]salon" or "#[cityname]barber"
3. Click "Tags" in search results
4. Browse posts → when you find a salon with good engagement, tap their profile
5. Check their bio for a website link or email
6. If only an Instagram contact button: note it as "Instagram only — DM as outreach"

HOW TO FIND THEIR EMAIL FROM THEIR INSTAGRAM:
- Check the bio (many put email directly there)
- Tap "Contact" button on their profile → if it shows an email, copy it
- Go to their Linktree (if they have one) — often links to their website where you'll find an email

QUALIFYING CHECKLIST — SALON:
[ ] Independent (not a chain)
[ ] Active Instagram or Facebook
[ ] Takes enquiries via DM, website form, or phone
[ ] No Fresha/Booksy/Treatwell widget
[ ] Email or Instagram contact found
[ ] 4+ star Google rating
→ If most ticked: ADD TO SHEET

OUTREACH TIP FOR SALONS:
Many salon owners are on their phones during the day between clients. A short, conversational email works well. Reference something specific from their Instagram if possible (e.g. "saw your post about the balayage you did last week — nice work").

========================================================
NICHE 3 — ESTATE AGENTS
========================================================

WHAT MAKES A GOOD PROSPECT:
- Independent estate agent (not a national chain like Foxtons, Purplebricks, or William H Brown)
- Has a Rightmove or Zoopla profile with active listings
- Small office — 2–10 staff (owner is likely still directly involved in sales)
- Website with a contact/valuation request form
- No obvious chatbot or live chat on their website
- Operates in a competitive local market (more listings = more competition = more need for fast responses)

GOOGLE MAPS SEARCH QUERIES:
1. "estate agent" [town/city name]
2. "independent estate agent" [town/city name]
3. "letting agent" [town/city name]
4. "property sales" [town/city name]
5. "estate agents near [postcode]"

RIGHTMOVE METHOD (excellent for finding agents):
1. Go to rightmove.co.uk
2. Search for properties in your target area
3. On a property listing, click the agent's name/logo
4. This takes you to the agent's Rightmove profile
5. Look for "Visit website" button → go to their actual website
6. Check for contact form and live chat as above

ZOOPLA METHOD:
1. zoopla.co.uk → search a town
2. Filter: "For sale" → click any listing → go to the agent profile
3. Same process as Rightmove above

HOW TO FIND THE OWNER'S EMAIL:
Estate agents often have a directory of staff on their website (e.g. "Meet the team"). The owner is usually listed as Director, Principal, or Founder.

Formats to try:
- [firstname]@[agencyname].co.uk
- [firstname].[lastname]@[agencyname].co.uk
- info@[agencyname].co.uk (if you can't find a personal one)
- Try LinkedIn → search the agency name + "director" → find their name → guess email format

QUALIFYING CHECKLIST — ESTATE AGENT:
[ ] Independent (not a national brand)
[ ] Has own website with contact/valuation form
[ ] No live chat widget visible on website
[ ] Active Rightmove or Zoopla profile
[ ] Operates in a competitive local market
[ ] Owner's email found
→ If most ticked: ADD TO SHEET

OUTREACH TIP FOR AGENTS:
Estate agents are used to being pitched — they get a lot of cold contact. Keep your email ultra-short and lead with the pain point (slow response to valuations = losing vendors to competitors). Reference the specific area they cover if possible.

========================================================
USING YELL.COM TO FIND LEADS
========================================================

Yell.com is the UK's leading local business directory. It's an excellent free source of prospects because:
- It covers all niches in one place
- Most listings include business name, phone, address, and website
- You can filter by niche and location
- No account needed to browse

HOW TO SEARCH ON YELL.COM:

1. Go to yell.com
2. In the "What?" box: enter your niche (e.g. "MOT Centre", "Hair Salon", "Estate Agent")
3. In the "Where?" box: enter a UK city or postcode
4. Press Search

5. Results appear as a list. For each result you can see:
   - Business name
   - Rating and number of reviews
   - Address
   - Phone number
   - Website link (if they have one)
   - Sometimes a direct email

MANUALLY SCRAPING YELL.COM (no tools needed):

1. Open Yell search results for your niche + city
2. Open each listing in a new tab (Ctrl+Click to batch open 10 at once)
3. For each listing, note down in your Google Sheet:
   - business_name
   - phone (in case email isn't available)
   - website (click through to find their email)
   - city and niche
4. Click their website link → find their email (Contact page, footer, About page)
5. Close the tab and move to the next

YELL PRO TIP — Using the "No website" filter:
On Yell search results, some businesses are listed without a website. These are actually BETTER prospects because:
- They're less digitally savvy (easier sell)
- They're more likely to be handling enquiries manually
- However, you'll need to find an email address some other way (see below)

For businesses without a website:
- Phone them (can be very effective — say "I found you on Yell and wanted to ask about your website enquiries")
- Search their business name on Facebook — many small businesses use Facebook as their website
- Check Google Maps listing — sometimes an email is listed there

HOW MANY LEADS CAN YELL GIVE YOU?
A single search for "garage" in "Birmingham" returns 50+ results.
Across 3–4 cities × 3 niches = 400–600 raw listings.
After qualifying, expect 15–25% to meet your criteria = 60–150 qualified prospects.

That's more than enough to test this model before investing in any paid tools.

BATCH APPROACH FOR EFFICIENCY:
Rather than working one result at a time:
1. Open Yell → search niche + city
2. Open 10 results simultaneously in new tabs
3. Work through each tab: find email → record in sheet → close tab
4. Repeat for next 10 results
5. This process: ~10 leads per 30 minutes once you're in the flow

========================================================
RECORDING LEADS IN YOUR GOOGLE SHEET
========================================================

Set up your Google Sheet using the headers from leads-template.csv. Here's how to fill it in efficiently:

IMPORT THE CSV TEMPLATE:
1. Open Google Sheets → File → Import → Upload → select leads-template.csv
2. Import type: Replace spreadsheet
3. Separator: Comma
4. Your template is now loaded with example rows

SUGGESTED COLUMN FORMATTING:
- business_name: Bold (makes the sheet easier to scan)
- status: Use Data Validation → dropdown list → values: new, emailed, replied-positive, replied-negative, closed-won, closed-lost
- sent_date: Format as date (Format → Number → Date)
- followup_count: Number, default 0
- deal_value: Currency (GBP)

ADD CONDITIONAL FORMATTING FOR STATUS:
1. Select the "status" column
2. Format → Conditional formatting
3. Add rules:
   - "emailed" → light blue background
   - "replied-positive" → light green background
   - "replied-negative" → light red background
   - "closed-won" → dark green background + bold
   - "closed-lost" → grey background

DAILY WORKFLOW:
Morning (15 min):
- Update "days_since_email" for any active leads
- Move any due follow-ups to your to-send list for today
- Record any new replies from yesterday

During prospecting (20–30 min):
- Add new leads from Yell/Google Maps research
- Mark all new rows as status = "new"

After sending emails:
- Update status to "emailed"
- Set sent_date to today's date
- Set followup_count to 0

When a reply comes in:
- Update replied = "Yes"
- Change status to replied-positive or replied-negative
- Add notes (what did they say?)

A WORKING WEEK PLAN FOR 50 LEADS:

Day 1 — Garages (3 UK cities, Google Maps + Yell)
Target: 10 new leads added, 10 emails sent

Day 2 — Hair Salons (2 UK cities, Google Maps + Instagram)
Target: 10 new leads added, 10 emails sent (including Day 1 + Day 3 follow-ups = 10+2)

Day 3 — Estate Agents (2 UK cities, Rightmove + Google Maps)
Target: 10 new leads added, 10 emails sent

Day 4 — Mixed (expand to 2 new niches: Plumbers + Restaurants)
Target: 10 new leads added, 10 emails sent + Day 3 follow-ups for Day 1 sends

Day 5 — Review + Follow-ups
- No new prospecting today
- Send Day 3 follow-ups for Day 2 sends
- Send Day 7 follow-ups for anyone who hasn't replied from Week 1
- Review what's working: which niches are opening/replying most?
- Refine your email subject line or opening based on results

By end of Day 5: 50 new leads in your sheet, 50 emails sent, follow-up sequences running.

TOOLS YOU DO NOT NEED (SAVE YOUR MONEY)
========================================================

You DO NOT need any of these to get your first 50 leads:
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator (£60–100/month) — use free LinkedIn search instead
- Apollo.io, Hunter.io Pro, Lusha — free tiers are enough for 50 leads
- Instantly, Lemlist, or other email sequencing tools — use Gmail + your Google Sheet manually
- Data scraping software — Yell + Google Maps manual research is faster for 50 leads

Once you have 5+ paying clients and want to scale to 500+ leads/month, revisit these tools. For now, save the cash.
