========================================================
GMAIL SETUP GUIDE — COLD OUTREACH + N8N OAUTH
AI Lead Reply Bot | Complete Step-by-Step
========================================================

OVERVIEW
---------
This guide covers five things:
(a) Creating a dedicated outreach Gmail account
(b) Warming it up over 3 days before you start sending cold emails
(c) Setting up a Gmail filter and label for tracking replies
(d) Gmail settings to maximise deliverability
(e) Connecting Gmail to n8n via OAuth for automation

Estimated setup time: 45–60 minutes total
(Warming period is 3 days but requires minimal effort per day)

========================================================
PART A — CREATING YOUR OUTREACH GMAIL ACCOUNT
========================================================

WHY A SEPARATE ACCOUNT?
Using your main Gmail for cold outreach risks your primary inbox getting flagged as spam. A dedicated outreach account keeps things clean and protects your reputation.

STEP 1: Choose a good email address
Format options (choose one that looks professional):
- [yourname]outreach@gmail.com
- hello.[yourname].ai@gmail.com
- [yourname].automation@gmail.com
- [yourname]aiuk@gmail.com

AVOID:
- noreply@
- info@
- admin@
- Anything with numbers at the end (e.g. joebloggs123@gmail.com — looks spammy)

STEP 2: Create the account
1. Go to accounts.google.com → "Create account" → "For my personal use"
2. Enter the email address you've chosen
3. Set a strong password (use a password manager)
4. Do NOT skip the phone verification — Gmail requires it for new accounts and it helps with deliverability
5. Fill in your real name (this appears as the sender name in emails)
   Sender name to use: "[YOUR NAME] | AI Automation" or just "[YOUR NAME]"

STEP 3: Complete the profile
1. Add a profile picture — a professional headshot improves reply rates significantly
   Tip: Use a real photo of yourself, not a logo
2. Go to Gmail → Settings (cog) → See all settings → General
3. Set "Send mail as": [YOUR NAME] <[yourname]outreach@gmail.com>

STEP 4: Add a signature
Go to Settings → General → Signature → Create new

Suggested signature:
---
[YOUR NAME]
AI Automation for UK Local Businesses
[YOUR_SALES_PAGE_URL]
---

Keep it short. No images in the signature for cold outreach — images trigger spam filters.

========================================================
PART B — WARMING UP YOUR GMAIL ACCOUNT (3 DAYS)
========================================================

WHY YOU MUST WARM UP
A brand new Gmail account sending cold emails immediately will be flagged as spam and potentially suspended. Warming = teaching Google that this is a real, human-used account.

WARMING SCHEDULE:

--- DAY 1 (Day of account creation) ---

Morning:
[ ] Send emails to 3–5 real email addresses you control or trust (family, friends, colleagues, your other Gmail accounts)
    Subject: "Just testing a new email — ignore this"
    Body: "Hi [Name], just testing out this new email address. Hope you're well!"
[ ] Have those people REPLY to your email (this is important — replies signal legitimacy)
[ ] Reply to their replies

Afternoon:
[ ] Send 2–3 more emails to different contacts
[ ] Open and read emails in your inbox — click on links, interact naturally
[ ] Mark a couple of emails as "Important" (star them)
[ ] Subscribe to 2–3 legitimate newsletters (e.g. HubSpot Blog, Marketing Week, Campaign Monitor)

--- DAY 2 ---

Morning:
[ ] Read and click links in the newsletters you subscribed to
[ ] Reply to any emails from Day 1
[ ] Send 5 more emails to real contacts — these can be slightly more "businessy" in nature
    e.g. "Hey, I'm setting up a new outreach email for my AI automation work — this is a test"
[ ] Spend 10 minutes reading and scrolling through emails (mimics human behaviour)

Afternoon:
[ ] Forward an email to yourself from your main account
[ ] Mark one email as "Not spam" (go to Spam folder, pull something out — shows Gmail you use spam controls)
[ ] Add 2 contacts to your Google Contacts from the account

--- DAY 3 ---

Morning:
[ ] Send 5–10 emails — mix of personal and slightly professional
[ ] Have at least 3 replies land in your inbox today
[ ] Reply to 2–3 of them
[ ] Open and interact with at least 10 emails in your inbox
[ ] Click "Mark as important" on 2 emails

Afternoon:
[ ] Compose and send one "test" cold email to yourself (your other Gmail) — exactly as you'll be sending to leads
[ ] Read it from the recipient side — check it doesn't look spammy, check formatting is correct
[ ] Draft 5–10 real cold emails ready to send tomorrow (save as drafts, don't send yet)

BY THE END OF DAY 3, YOUR ACCOUNT IS READY.

ONGOING WARM-UP RULES (first 30 days of sending):
- Week 1: Max 15 cold emails per day
- Week 2: Max 25 cold emails per day
- Week 3+: Max 40–50 cold emails per day
- Always keep a mix of warm emails going (regular inbox activity) alongside cold sends
- Never send more than 50 cold emails in one day from a Gmail account
- If you see open rates drop suddenly, slow down for 2–3 days

PART C — GMAIL FILTER FOR TRACKING REPLIES
========================================================

You want all replies to your cold emails automatically labelled so you can track them easily.

STEP 1: Create a label
1. Gmail left sidebar → scroll down → "+ Create new label"
2. Label name: outreach-replies
3. Optionally create sub-labels:
   - outreach-replies/interested
   - outreach-replies/not-interested
   - outreach-replies/follow-up-needed

STEP 2: Create the filter
1. Gmail → Settings → See all settings → Filters and Blocked Addresses → "Create a new filter"
2. In the "To" field, enter your outreach email address: [yourname]outreach@gmail.com
3. Click "Create filter with this search"
4. Tick: "Apply the label" → select "outreach-replies"
5. Tick: "Never send it to Spam" (important!)
6. Tick: "Also apply filter to matching conversations"
7. Click "Create filter"

Now every email that arrives in this inbox is labelled automatically. You can then manually sub-label them as interested/not-interested.

STEP 3: Create a filter to avoid sending follow-ups to people who replied
In your Gmail outreach tool or manually — before sending a Day 3/7/14 follow-up:
1. Search Gmail for: from:[their email address]
2. If they've replied, skip that person in your follow-up round

========================================================
PART D — GMAIL SETTINGS TO OPTIMISE DELIVERABILITY
========================================================

These settings reduce the chance of your emails landing in spam.

SETTING 1: Enable Plain Text Sending
For cold outreach, plain text emails get higher deliverability than HTML-heavy ones.
- When composing cold emails, use the default compose window (not rich text if possible)
- Avoid: bold text, bullet points, images, coloured text, multiple fonts in cold emails
- Your email should look like it was typed by a human in Notepad

SETTING 2: Disable email tracking for initial sends
If you use a tracking tool (e.g. Mailtrack), disable open tracking for the first 2 weeks of a new account — tracking pixels can trigger spam filters on new domains.

SETTING 3: Reply-to address
Go to Settings → Accounts → "Send mail as" → Edit info
Set a Reply-To address of [yourname]outreach@gmail.com (same account) — this is fine, just ensures replies land in the right place.

SETTING 4: IMAP / POP access (needed for n8n)
1. Gmail → Settings → See all settings → Forwarding and POP/IMAP
2. Enable IMAP: "Enable IMAP" → Save Changes
3. This allows n8n and other tools to read your inbox programmatically

SETTING 5: Two-factor authentication
1. Go to myaccount.google.com → Security → 2-Step Verification → Enable
2. Use an authenticator app (Google Authenticator or Authy) — NOT SMS
3. This is required before you can generate an OAuth token for n8n

SETTING 6: Less secure apps / App passwords (if using older integrations)
If n8n requires it:
1. myaccount.google.com → Security → App passwords
2. Create an app password for "Mail" on "Windows computer" (or your OS)
3. Copy the 16-character password — use this in n8n instead of your regular password
NOTE: OAuth (below) is more secure and preferred over app passwords.

SETTING 7: Sending limits awareness
Gmail free accounts: 500 emails/day hard limit
Gmail Workspace (paid): 2,000 emails/day
For cold outreach, stay well under these limits (max 50/day) to avoid suspension.

DAILY HYGIENE CHECKLIST (takes 5 min/day):
[ ] Read and reply to any positive replies first thing
[ ] Label any replies (interested / not interested)
[ ] Update your Google Sheet (leads-template.csv) with replied = Yes and notes
[ ] Archive emails you've dealt with
[ ] Check Spam folder daily — move any legitimate replies out

PART E — GMAIL OAUTH IN N8N
========================================================

This connects your Gmail account to n8n so your workflows can send emails, read the inbox, and trigger on new replies automatically.

STEP 1: Enable Gmail API in Google Cloud Console

1. Go to console.cloud.google.com
2. Sign in with your outreach Gmail account
3. Create a new project:
   - Click the project dropdown (top bar) → "New Project"
   - Name: "n8n-gmail-integration" → Create
4. Enable the Gmail API:
   - Navigation menu → APIs & Services → Library
   - Search "Gmail API" → Click it → "Enable"
5. Enable the Google Sheets API (while you're here):
   - Library → Search "Google Sheets API" → Enable

STEP 2: Create OAuth 2.0 Credentials

1. APIs & Services → Credentials → "+ Create Credentials" → "OAuth client ID"
2. If prompted, configure the OAuth consent screen first:
   - User type: External
   - App name: "n8n Automation [YOUR NAME]"
   - User support email: your outreach Gmail address
   - Developer contact: same email
   - Scopes → Add scopes:
     - gmail.readonly
     - gmail.send
     - gmail.compose
     - gmail.modify
     - (If using Sheets) spreadsheets (read/write)
   - Test users: Add your outreach Gmail address
   - Save and continue
3. Back to Create OAuth client ID:
   - Application type: Web application
   - Name: "n8n Gmail Client"
   - Authorised redirect URIs: Add the following:
     - https://[your-n8n-instance]/rest/oauth2-credential/callback
     (If n8n Cloud: https://[your-workspace].app.n8n.cloud/rest/oauth2-credential/callback)
     (If local testing: http://localhost:5678/rest/oauth2-credential/callback)
4. Click "Create"
5. COPY and SAVE:
   - Client ID (looks like: 123456789-abc123.apps.googleusercontent.com)
   - Client Secret (looks like: GOCSPX-abc123xyz)

STEP 3: Add Gmail Credentials in n8n

1. Open your n8n instance
2. Left menu → Credentials → "+ Add Credential" → Search "Gmail"
3. Select "Gmail OAuth2 API"
4. Fill in:
   - Client ID: paste from Step 2
   - Client Secret: paste from Step 2
5. Click "Sign in with Google" → A Google window opens
6. Select your outreach Gmail account
7. Grant all requested permissions
8. You'll be redirected back to n8n with "Connected" shown in green

STEP 4: Test the connection

1. Create a simple n8n workflow:
   - Manual Trigger → Gmail node (Send Email)
   - To: your personal email
   - Subject: "n8n Gmail test"
   - Body: "This is a test from n8n"
2. Run the workflow
3. Check your personal inbox — if the email arrives, the connection works

STEP 5: Set up the Gmail Trigger (to catch replies)

1. In your outreach workflow, add a "Gmail Trigger" node
2. Credential: select the Gmail OAuth credential you just created
3. Trigger on: "New Email"
4. Optional filters:
   - Label: outreach-replies (to only trigger on labelled reply emails)
   - Search filter: is:unread (to only trigger on unread emails)
5. Polling interval: Every 5 minutes (or 15 minutes to be safe)
6. Connect this trigger to your "reply handling" workflow:
   - Parse the sender's name, email, and reply content
   - Update the Google Sheet (set replied = Yes, add reply content to notes)
   - Send yourself a WhatsApp notification: "REPLY from [sender name] at [business name]"
