========================================================
TALLY ONBOARDING FORM — BUILD INSTRUCTIONS
AI Lead Reply Bot | Client Onboarding
========================================================

OVERVIEW
---------
This form is sent to clients immediately after they pay via Stripe. It collects everything your n8n workflow needs to build and configure their AI bot. The form is built in Tally.so (free plan is sufficient for this).

Tally fields map directly to variables used in your n8n workflow, so field names must match EXACTLY as written below.

========================================================
STEP 1 — CREATE YOUR TALLY ACCOUNT
========================================================

1. Go to tally.so → Sign up (free)
2. Create a new form → click "New form" → choose "Start from scratch"
3. Give the form an internal name: "AI Bot Onboarding — [YOUR NAME]"
   (This is internal only — clients don't see this)

========================================================
STEP 3 — ALL FORM FIELDS
========================================================

Build these fields in the exact order listed. Field names (in brackets) are the variable names your n8n workflow will read — do not change them.

-----------------------------------------------------------
PAGE 1: ABOUT YOUR BUSINESS
-----------------------------------------------------------

FIELD 1
Internal name: business_name
Label: What's the name of your business?
Type: Short text (single line)
Required: YES
Placeholder: e.g. City Auto Centre
Description: This is how your bot will introduce itself to customers.

FIELD 2
Internal name: owner_name
Label: Your name (or the name customers know you by)
Type: Short text
Required: YES
Placeholder: e.g. Darren
Description: We may use this in the bot's responses when signing off.

FIELD 3
Internal name: owner_email
Label: Your email address
Type: Email
Required: YES
Placeholder: e.g. darren@cityautocentre.co.uk
Description: This is where we'll send updates and your weekly lead report.

FIELD 4
Internal name: owner_phone
Label: Your mobile number (for WhatsApp lead notifications)
Type: Phone number
Required: YES
Placeholder: e.g. 07712 345678
Description: We'll send you a WhatsApp notification every time a qualified lead comes in.

FIELD 5
Internal name: business_niche
Label: What type of business are you?
Type: Dropdown (single select)
Required: YES
Options:
  - Car Garage / MOT Centre
  - Hair Salon / Barber
  - Restaurant / Takeaway
  - Estate Agent
  - Letting Agency
  - Plumber / Electrician
  - Dental Practice
  - Personal Trainer / Gym
  - Other (please specify below)

FIELD 6
Internal name: business_niche_other
Label: If you selected "Other", please describe your business type
Type: Short text
Required: NO
Placeholder: e.g. Dog grooming, physiotherapy, driving school

FIELD 7
Internal name: business_address
Label: What's your business address or area you cover?
Type: Long text
Required: YES
Placeholder: e.g. 14 High Street, Wolverhampton, WV1 1AB — or — We cover all of West Yorkshire

FIELD 8
Internal name: website_url
Label: What's your website URL? (if you have one)

Required: NO

========================================================
FIELD MAPPING REFERENCE FOR N8N
========================================================

When your n8n workflow reads the Tally webhook, it will receive a JSON object.
Here are the key field names to reference:

data.fields[0].value  → business_name
data.fields[1].value  → owner_name
data.fields[2].value  → owner_email
data.fields[3].value  → owner_phone
data.fields[4].value  → business_niche
data.fields[6].value  → business_address
data.fields[7].value  → website_url
data.fields[8].value  → services_list
data.fields[11].value → opening_hours
data.fields[12].value → booking_process
data.fields[15].value → tone_of_voice
data.fields[17].value → owner_sign_off
data.fields[18].value → frequently_asked_questions
data.fields[19].value → channels_to_connect

NOTE: Tally numbers fields sequentially from 0. If you add or remove fields,
re-check the index numbers in your n8n workflow. Alternatively, use Tally's
field labels in the webhook payload and match by label name instead of index.
